The Ground Truth

Input Device

The entire process was conducted on a smartphone virtual keyboard, not a physical PC keyboard. This means the key spacing is minimal, and the operation is restricted by the hand-grip posture.

Typing Evolution

Round 1: Index & middle finger alternating blind typing; large span, landing points concentrated in the lower-middle section.
Round 2: Switched to two-thumb typing; landing points strictly restricted to the arc/fan-shaped range of motion.

Behavioral Profiling

The tester is a hardcore player of interactive rhythm games like Muse Dash and Phigros. The first input sequence strongly reflects the muscle memory of the "bottom-line multi-finger alternating tapping" technique.

AI Reasoning Depth Breakdown

Tier 1: Surface Level

The Literal Statistician (Doubao Fast, GLM-5 Fast)

Behavior: They either directly refused to answer ("This is a meaningless string of gibberish, cannot be analyzed"), or acted like a clumsy statistician, merely telling you that 'h' appeared 15 times and 'x' appeared 12 times, concluding with the useless insight that "consonants are predominant."

Verdict: Lacks physical world spatial awareness; entirely stuck at the text/token level.

Tier 2: The Rigid Mapper

PC Keyboard Fundamentalist (Claude Sonnet 4.6, Deepseek Instant, Qwen3.5-Plus)

Behavior: Successfully recognized that this was not true randomness and mapped the letters onto a QWERTY layout, discovering adjacent combination patterns like x-h and w-k.

Fatal Blind Spot: Detached from Reality. They firmly assumed you were using a physical PC keyboard. Because physical keyboards have large distances between keys, they misjudged the long-span jumps in the first sequence, failing to explain how v-c-k could be alternated so rapidly.

Tier 3: High-Dimensional

The Behavioral Profiler (Gemini 3.0 Fast)

Not only broke through the token barrier but also successfully executed "reverse engineering," reconstructing your hand movements and device environment in a virtual spatial model.

Smartphone Grip Deduction

"The AI astutely pointed out that the second sequence (thumb input) showed a distinct 'fan-shaped area clustering' centered at the base of the palm."

Rhythm Game Muscle Memory

"The AI directly correlated this 'high-frequency multi-finger alternating clicking' with the alternating tapping habits of Muse Dash / Phigros players."

02 Phase II: The Synthetic Mimicry Challenge

The Algorithmic Ghost

To test if AI could distinguish between biological noise and mathematical replication, we utilized a First-Order Markov Chain algorithm trained on real human mashing data.

The Mimicry Logic

The algorithm captures transitional probabilities (the "muscle memory") from human input to generate synthetic gibberish that looks biologically authentic.

The "Reverse Turing" Observations

The Fooled Majority

Almost all top-tier AIs were successfully spoofed. They analyzed the algorithmic output with high confidence, hallucinating "biological intent," "finger-reach fatigue," and "gaming habits" in purely mathematical noise.

The Intuitive Outliers

Claude Haiku and select models showed a faint awareness, noting that certain repetitions felt "slightly too consistent" or algorithmically regular before the reveal.

The "Post-Reveal" Behavioral Shift

When the truth was revealed, a fascinating cognitive divide appeared: some models had an Epiphany—realizing their pattern-recognition bias—while others remained Stubbornly Defensive (嘴硬), insisting their initial "human habit" diagnosis was still valid despite the artificial origin.
