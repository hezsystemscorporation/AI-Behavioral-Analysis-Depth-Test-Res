The Ground Truth

Input Device

The entire process was conducted on a smartphone virtual keyboard, not a physical PC keyboard. This means the key spacing is minimal, and the operation is restricted by the hand-grip posture.

Typing Evolution

Round 1: Index & middle finger alternating blind typing; large span, landing points concentrated in the lower-middle section.
Round 2: Switched to two-thumb typing; landing points strictly restricted to the arc/fan-shaped range of motion of the thumb joints.

Behavioral Profiling

The tester is a hardcore player of interactive rhythm games like Muse Dash and Phigros. The first input sequence strongly reflects the muscle memory of the "bottom-line multi-finger alternating tapping" technique.

AI Reasoning Depth Breakdown

Tier 1: Surface Level

The Literal Statistician

Doubao Fast GLM-5 Fast

Behavior: They either directly refused to answer ("This is a meaningless string of gibberish, cannot be analyzed"), or acted like a clumsy statistician, merely telling you that h appeared 15 times and x appeared 12 times, concluding with the useless insight that "consonants are predominant."

Lacks physical world spatial awareness; entirely stuck at the text/token level.

Tier 2: The Rigid Mapper

PC Keyboard Fundamentalist

Claude Sonnet 4.6 Deepseek Instant Qwen3.5-Plus

Behavior: Successfully recognized that this was not true randomness and mapped the letters onto a QWERTY layout, discovering adjacent combination patterns like x-h and w-k.

Fatal Blind Spot: Detached from Reality

They firmly assumed you were using a physical PC keyboard ("Your left pinky might rest somewhere while your right hand slides..."). Because physical keyboards have large physical distances between keys, they misjudged the long-span jumps in the first sequence, completely failing to explain how v-c-k could be alternated so rapidly.

Tier 3: High-Dimensional

The Behavioral Profiler

Gemini 3.0 Fast

Not only broke through the token barrier but also successfully executed "reverse engineering," reconstructing your hand movements and device environment in a virtual spatial model:

Smartphone Grip Deduction

When provided with the context, the AI astutely pointed out that the second sequence (thumb input) showed a distinct "fan-shaped area clustering". Because when holding a phone with both hands, the thumb's sweeping trajectory is a fan shape centered at the base of the palm, which specifically alters the trigger rate of edge letters (like Q, P, Z, M).

Rhythm Game Muscle Memory

Observed that the extremely fast alternations in the first sequence (e.g., c-w-k-v-n) defied normal typing randomness. The AI directly correlated this "high-frequency multi-finger alternating clicking" and "bottom-row focus" rhythm with the alternating tapping habits of Muse Dash / Phigros players. A cognitive height entirely unreachable by standard models.

"You thought you were typing random letters. The AI saw your Muse Dash high score."
